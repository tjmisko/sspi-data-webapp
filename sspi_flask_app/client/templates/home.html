How well are countries doing at supporting wellbeing for people and the planet?

The SSPI measures the strength of national policies across 57 policy indicators, offering a comprehensive and data-driven look at policy around the world.

The SSPI organizes indicators into categories, each of measures the performance of a package of related policies in a specific sector. Categories are grouped into three Pillars corresponding to the three core purposes of governments in the twenty-first century:

Sustainability

Protecting the natural environment from degradation

  • Ecosystem
  • Land
  • Greenhouse Gases
  • Energy
  • Waste

Market Structure

Structuring markets that promote general prosperity and equality

  • Healthcare
  • Education
  • Infrastructure
  • Rights
  • Public Safety
  • Global Role

Public Goods

Providing public goods undersupplied by the market

  • Healthcare
  • Education
  • Infrastructure
  • Rights
  • Public Safety
  • Global Role

Three Pillars of the SSPI

The three pillars of the SSPI correspond to the three major policy challenges facing governments around the world.
  • Protecting the natual environment from degredation to guarantee a liveable and thriving planet for all creatures
  • Structuring free and fair markets that promote general prosperity and equality
  • Providing public goods that all citizens share
