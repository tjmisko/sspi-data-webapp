How well are countries doing at guaranteeing wellbeing for people and the planet?

The SSPI assembles data measuring country performance across 57 Policy Indicators, offering a comprehensive and data-driven look at policy around the world.

The SSPI groups these Policy Indicators into sixteen Categories, each of measures the performance of a package of related policies in a specific sector. The Categories are grouped into three Pillars corresponding to the three core purposes of governments in the twenty-first century: protecting the planet (Sustainability), structuring markets (Market Structure), and providing Public Goods.

Three Pillars of the SSPI

The three pillars of the SSPI correspond to the three major policy challenges facing governments around the world.
  • Protecting the natual environment from degredation to guarantee a liveable and thriving planet for all creatures
  • Structuring free and fair markets that promote general prosperity and equality
  • Providing public goods that all citizens share
