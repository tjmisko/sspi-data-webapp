How would you measure policy according to your values?

The SSPI methodology puts shared values first, then measures how well policy across countries aligns with those values. In foregrounding values, however, we recognize that different people, different communities, different countries, and different cultures have different values, so they may evaluate policy differently than we do.

Our customization tool provides a framework for applying the SSPI methodology beginning from your own values. It allows individuals to modify how policies are scored and grouped and to explore how those changes affect the scores and conclusions at all levels of the analysis.

Using the Customization Tool

The customization tool allows you to modify the the organization and scoring of the SSPI and to see how those changes affect the overall scores. Use the tool to:

  • Adjust how indicators are scored by adjusting their underlying datasets, the numerical values of Goalposts, and the functional form of the Score Function for each indicator.
  • Create custom policy indicators with access to the full database of underlying datasets prepared for the SSPI, or with your own custom data uploaded through the data upload portal.
  • Change how indicators, categories, and pillars are grouped, scored, and named to create your own custom, data-driven policy index.
  • Compare your each of your changes ceteris paribus with the canonical SSPI to explore how differences in values change the conclusions about the quality of national policies.
  • Save and share your customized configuration of the SSPI, its scores, and the summary report.

To generate custom scores, we require that you create a free account for saving your configuration and protecting our API. Scoring is a computationally intensive operation involving large volumes of data; we therefore limit the number of score requests you can make to ten per day.
